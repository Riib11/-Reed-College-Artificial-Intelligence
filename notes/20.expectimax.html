Probabilitic Games

Black Jack

The variant that we will work with is where you don't get to see any of the dealer's cards, and aces always count as 1s.

Probabilities:

Dealer:

$$ \begin{array}{r|l} \text{Hand} & Probability \\ \hline 21 & 12\% \\ 20 & 18\% \\ 19 & 13\% \\ 18 & 14\% \\ 17 & 15\% \\ \text{Bust} & 28\% \\ \end{array} $$

Player:

$$ \begin{array}{r|ccc|l} \text{Hand} & \text{Win} & \text{Draw} & \text{Loss} & \text{Expected Payout} \\ \hline 21 & 88\% & 12\% & 0\% & 0.88 \\ 20 & 7\% & 18\% & 12\% & 0.58 \\ 19 & 57\% & 13\% & 3\% & 0.27 \\ 18 & 43\% & 14\% & 43\% & 0.00 \\ 17 & 28\% & 15\% & 57\% & -0.29 \\ < 17 & 28\% & 0\% & 72\% & -0.44 \\ \text{Bust} & 0\% & 0\% & 100\% & -1 \end{array} $$

Represent the minimax tree as having two players: you and fate. Fate chooses the cards. But in the minimax algorithm, rather than using 'min' to choose for Fate, instead just calculate expected value.
