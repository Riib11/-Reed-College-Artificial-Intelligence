  • \(M_1 \dbot M_2\). This is wrong though! So this graph is wrong.

    • Genes

    $$ \begin{aligned} P(R=AB \mid T=A) &= \frac{P(R=AB, T=A)}{P(T=A)} \\ P(R=AB, T=A) &= \sum_{r^*} \sum_{s^*} \sum_{t^*} \sum_{m^*} \sum_{f^*} \sum_{s^*} P(R=AB, T=A, r^*, s^*, t^*, m, f, s) \end{aligned} $$

    but this has 7776 terms! So lets make it simpler

    $$ \begin{aligned} &= \sum \sum \sum \sum \sum P(r^*) P(s^*) P(r \mid r^*) P(m \mid r^*) P(f \mid s^*) P(s \mid s^*) P(t^* \mid m, f) P(t \mid t^*) \\ &= \sum \sum \sum \sum \sum g_1(r^*) g_2(s^*) g_3(r, r^*) g_4(m, r^*) g_5(f, s^*) g_6(s, s^*) g_7(t, m, f) g_8(t, t^*) \\ &= \sum_{m} \sum_{f} \sum_{t^*} \sum_{r^*} \sum_{s^*} \sum_{s} g_1(r^*) g_2(s^*) g_3(r, r^*) g_4(m, r^*) g_5(f, s^*) g_6(s, s^*) g_7(t, m, f) g_8(t, t^*) \\ &= \sum_{m} \sum_{f} \sum_{t^*} \sum_{r^*} \sum_{s^*} g_1(r^*) g_2(s^*) g_3(r, r^*) g_4(m, r^*) g_5(f, s^*) g_7(t, m, f) g_8(t, t^*) ( h_1(s^*) = \sum_s g_6(s, s^*) ) \\ &= \sum_{m} \sum_{t^*} \sum_{r^*} g_1(r^*) g_3(r, r^*) g_4(m, r^*) g_7(t, m, f) g_8(t, t^*) (h_2 = \sum_{s^*} g_2(s^*) g_5(f, s^*) h_1(s^*)) \\ &= \dots \t{keep pushing summation inwards} \dots \end{aligned} $$

    Where

    $$ \begin{aligned} h_1(s^*) &= \sum_s g_6(s, s^*) \\ h_2(f) &= \sum_{s^*} g_2(s^*) g_5(f, s^*) h_1(s^*) \\ h_3(r, m) &= \sum_{r^*} g_1(r^*) g_3(r, r^*) g_4(m, r^*) \\ h_4(t, m, f) &= \sum_{t^*} g_7(t, m, f) g_8(t, t^*) \\ h_5(t, m) &= \sum_{f^*} h_2(f) h_4(t, m, f) \\ h_6(r, t) &= \sum_m h_3(r, m) h_5(t, m) \\ \end{aligned} $$

    By evaluating these, you get the value of \(P(R=AB, T=A)\) in 414 operations! This is much less than calculating out all the sums originally.
