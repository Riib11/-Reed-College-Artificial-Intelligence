The Monty Hall problem

The Problem Setup

Setup:

  • 3 doors
  • 2 doors have bad prizes
  • 1 door has a really good prize

    • Play:

  • player chooses a door
  • host opens one of the remaining doors that has a bad prize, revealing that to the player
  • then the player can either stay or switch to the other remaining door

    • People used to strongly and popularly disagree about whether to switch or stay. For example, a woman that wrote for parade said that its better to switch, but a bunch of PhD's said she was wrong.

    Solution

    Turns out she's right.

    The probabilities are complicated to work out and use conditional dependence to show you're better off always switching.
