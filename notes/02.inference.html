Inference

Entails:

$$ a \vDash b := I(b) \subseteq (b) $$

Can define logical equivelance (\(a \equiv b\)) in terms of entails (\(\vDash\)) in this way:

$$ a \equiv b := (a \vDash b) \land (b \vDash a) $$

Prop 1. \(a \land (a \implies b) \vDash b\).

Proof.

$$ \begin{aligned} I(a \land (a \implies b)) &= I(a) \cap I(a \implies b) \\ &= I(a) \cap I(\lnot a \lor b) \\ &= I(a) \cap (I(\lnot a) \cup I(b)) \\ &= (I(a) \cap I(\lnot a)) \cup (I(a) \cup I(b)) \\ &= I(a) \cap I(b) \\ &\subseteq I(b) \\ &\implies a \land (a \implies b) \vDash b \end{aligned} $$

Prop 2. \(a \land b \equiv b \land a\).

Proof.

$$ \begin{aligned} I(a \land b) &= I(a) \cap I(b) \\ &= I(b) \cap I(a) \\ &= I(b \land b) \end{aligned} $$

Prop 3. \((P \implies B) \land P \vDash B\).

Proof.

$$ \begin{aligned} (P \implies B) \land P &\equiv P \land (P \implies B) &\text{[Prop 2]} \\ &\vDash B &\text{[Prop 1]} \end{aligned} $$

Theorem 1. \(\forall a,b \in \mathcal{L}(\Sigma), a \vDash b \iff I(a \land \lnot b) = \varnothing\)

Proof. Suppose \(a \vDash b\). Assume \(m \in I(a \land \lnot b)\). Thus \(m \in I(a)\) and \(m \in I(\lnot b)\), thus \(m \not\in I(b)\). Now \(a \vDash b\), then \(I(a) \subseteq I(b)\), so \(\not\exists m^\prime\) such that \(m^\prime \in I(a)\) and \(m^\prime \not\in I(b)\)!

For the other direction, suppoes \(I(a \land \lnot b) = \varnothing\). Thus \(I(a) \cap I(\lnot b) = \varnothing\). Now consider \(m \in M\). If not \(m \in I(a)\), then \(m \not\in I(\lnot b)\). So \(m \in I(b)\). Thus \(I(a) \subseteq I(b) \implies a \vDash b\).

Satisfiability. \(a\) is satisfiable if \(I(a) \not= \varnothing\).

Conjunctive Normal Form: \((\lnot P \lor F) \land (P \lor B) \land (\lnot b \lor \lnot F \lor \lnot P)\). The final term is the clause.

For example, in \(\Sigma = \{ A_1, \dots, A_n \}\), can have

$$ A_1 \lor \lnot A_3 \lor \lnot A_6 $$
