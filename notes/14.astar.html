UCS seems to always find the most optimal solution, but this is not always the case (as least, as efficiently as we'd like).

Definition. A completion path from state \(q \in Q\) as a sequence of transitions \((d_i, d_k)\) where \(\forall 1 \leq j \leq k, \delta_i \in \Delta,\) and \(\delta_i = (q_i, \sigma_i, q_{i+1})\), \(q_1 = q_0\), \(q_{k+1} \in F\).

Definition. The cost of a completion path is the sum of the weights of its transitions.

Definition. \(H^* : Q \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is the cost of the optimal completion path from \(q\). Then the hueristic function \(H : Q \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is admissible if

$$ \forall q \in Q, H(q) \leq H^*(q) $$

For memoized search, need something more:

Definition. \(H\) is consistent if

$$ \forall q \in F, H(q) = 0 \\ \forall q, q^\prime \in Q \text{ where } (q, \sigma, q^\prime) \in \Delta, H(q) \leq w(q, \sigma, q^\prime) + H(q^\prime) $$
